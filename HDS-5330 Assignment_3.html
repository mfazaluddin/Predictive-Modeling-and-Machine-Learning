1. Linear Regression Equation:
Age At Surgery = 82.30 + -10.96 * Gender + 0.21 * Race

This equation shows how Age At Surgery is related to Gender and Race. The negative coefficient for Gender (-10.96) suggests that, on average, males have surgery about 11 years earlier than females. The small positive coefficient for Race (0.21) indicates a slight increase in surgery age as the race category number increases, but this effect is much smaller compared to the gender effect.¶

#Linear Regression: DOB vs Age at Surgery
df['DOB'] = pd.to_datetime(df['DOB'])
birth_years = df['DOB'].dt.year.values.reshape(-1, 1)
surgery_ages = df['Age At Surgery'].values

model = LinearRegression()
model.fit(birth_years, surgery_ages)

plt.figure(figsize=(10, 6))
plt.scatter(birth_years, surgery_ages, alpha=0.6)
plt.plot(birth_years, model.predict(birth_years), color='red', linewidth=2)
plt.xlabel('Year of Birth')
plt.ylabel('Age at Time of Surgery')
plt.title('Relationship Between Birth Year and Age at Surgery')

plt.show()

The graph shows a strong link between a person's birth year and age at surgery. There's a clear downward trend from left to right, meaning people born earlier had surgery at an older age compared to those born later. This relationship is quite consistent, as seen by the blue dots clustering closely around the red line. This suggests that over time, people are having this particular surgery at younger ages, possibly due to changes in medical practices or earlier problem detection.¶
